HCI: VR Art Therapy

In my second year of university, I took a module in Human Computer Interaction. I was required to research and design a product that would benefit a community of people. I created a prototype of a VR Paintbrush which provided an immersive environment for users to explore their feelings via art therapy.

Design

Here is my final design for this prototype created in Blender. This product involved to side buttons and a touch pad for navigation and a trigger button for grabbing. Alongside this tool would be a navigation menu inside the 3D environment for users to have extra tools to work with. I drew inspiration from Goodgle Tilt Brush and Oculus Rift designs and tried ti make the design as accessible as possible.

Demo

Here is a demonstration of how to tool would be used in therapy environment also created in Blender. I enjoyed modelling this scene and learning how to use keyframes to create basic animations in thie software.
